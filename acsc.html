Aegis ClearTrace Standards Committee ACSC

Independent standards body maintaining the ClearTrace Protocol for transparent algorithmic trading audit trails.

ACSC defines, publishes, and governs the ClearTrace Protocol CTP so that trading firms, brokers, and regulators can rely on a common, verifiable format for audit logs.

Mission

The Aegis ClearTrace Standards Committee ACSC maintains an open, implementation neutral standard for capturing complete and tamper evident audit trails of algorithmic and AI driven trading systems.

  • Define the core ClearTrace Protocol CTP and its extensions
  • Align the protocol with global regulatory requirements and best practices
  • Ensure that logs remain verifiable, portable, and interoperable across vendors

Scope and responsibilities

ACSC focuses on the standard itself, not on any single product or vendor implementation.

  • Versioning and change control for the ClearTrace Protocol CTP
  • Definition of required and optional fields for each event type
  • Governance of security primitives such as hashing, signatures, and chain integrity
  • Guidelines for regulatory alignment for regimes such as MiFID II, ESMA, SEC, and others
  • Reference guidance for interoperability tests and conformance checks

Governance model

ACSC is designed as an independent standards body with a transparent governance process. The goal is to allow multiple firms to rely on the same protocol while avoiding vendor lock in.

  • Public specification hosted at spec.clear-trace.org
  • Documented change proposals and version history
  • Technical working groups for protocol extensions and domain specific profiles
  • Advisory input from trading firms, brokers, audit and compliance experts, and academia

Membership and participation

ACSC is currently in its founding phase. We welcome early participants who share the goal of making algorithmic trading more transparent and auditable.

Founding roles draft

  • Secretariat: Aegis ClearTrace Inc. operational support and publication of the specification
  • Technical maintainer: AI MQL LLC reference implementations and feedback from live systems

Target member categories

  • Prop trading firms and proprietary trading desks
  • Retail and institutional brokers
  • EA and algorithmic trading system vendors
  • RegTech and audit service providers
  • Researchers and academic partners

The membership program is in beta. If you are interested in contributing to the protocol or running internal pilots, please contact the secretariat.

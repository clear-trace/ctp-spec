What is ACC?

Aegis ClearTrace Certified (ACC) is the world’s first certification program focused on the transparency of algorithmic and AI-driven trading systems. It evaluates whether a trading system generates verifiable audit logs following the ClearTrace Protocol (CTP) — an open standard for immutable decision and execution trails.

ACC functions as the official certification arm operated by Aegis ClearTrace Inc., while the underlying protocol (CTP) is governed by the Aegis ClearTrace Standards Committee (ACSC).

Why ACC matters

Global regulators increasingly require algorithmic trading firms to maintain detailed audit trails, including MiFID II (EU), SEC Rule 17a-4 (US), FINRA, FCA (UK), MAS (Singapore), DFSA (Dubai), SFC (Hong Kong), and SEBI (India). However, no international standard existed for capturing algorithmic decision processes in a verifiable format.

ACC fills this gap by certifying trading systems that adhere to CTP — ensuring that every Signal → Order → Execution → Result is captured in a provable, tamper-evident structure. This creates a transparent, conflict-free environment for Prop firms, brokers, regulators, and users.

Certification Levels (aligned with CTP Compliance Tiers)

ACC uses the same tier model defined in the ClearTrace Protocol v1.0.

  • Platinum — HFT, exchanges, ultra-low latency systems. Full PTPv2 sync, SBE format, on-chain anchoring every 10 minutes.
  • Gold — Prop firms and institutional algo desks. NTP sync, JSON + signature, local anchoring every 1 hour.
  • Silver — Retail MT4/MT5 algos, EA vendors. Best-effort timestamping, delegated signature, 24h anchoring.

ACC Requirements

To obtain ACC certification, a system must:

  • Output audit logs fully compliant with ClearTrace Protocol (CTP-CORE + CTP-SEC)
  • Implement hash-chained events (PrevHash, EventHash)
  • Include cryptographic signatures (ECDSA/Ed25519/RSA)
  • Support Merkle Root anchoring at the tier-appropriate interval
  • Provide complete history of Signals, Orders, Acks, Executions, Errors, and Closes
  • Submit logs for validation by the Aegis Judge Engine

Certification Process

  1. Registration & NDA
  2. Submission of system description & log samples
  3. Automated verification through Aegis Judge Engine
  4. Manual review for protocol conformance
  5. ACC Certification issuance + badge
  6. Annual renewal with updated logs
